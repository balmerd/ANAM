Welcome to the Alameda Naval Air Museum website.

Our museum is home to a unique collection of artifacts and exhibits emphasizing the role of the Alameda Naval Air Station in the defense of our nation and as an integral part of the local community for over 50 years. Long before the base was commissioned in 1940, Alameda Point was the home of industry and early aviation pioneers. In the late 1800's, twenty-mule teams pulled carts laden with borax to a processing plant located here and connection to the Transcontinental Railroad. Later, the Wright Brothers, Curtis-Wright Aviation Company, civilian aviators and the Army Air Corps called Alameda Point home. But this is just the beginning of the story...

Come and see the history in your backyard at the Alameda Naval Air Museum.
